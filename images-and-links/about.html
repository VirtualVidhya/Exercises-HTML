From a small network at U.S. research labs to today's global mesh, the Internet's history is a story of innovation and collaboration that reshaped communication.

Today's Data Centers

Massive data centers now house millions of servers, storing cloud services, websites, and apps that power everything from social media to online banking.

Key Milestones in Internet History

  1. 1969: ARPANET Goes Live
    • Connected four university nodes
    • Proved that remote computers could exchange data
  2. 1983: TCP/IP Becomes Standard
    • Established the universal language for all networks
    • Laid the groundwork for a truly global Internet
  3. 1991: World Wide Web Debuts
    • Tim Berners-Lee introduces HTML and HTTP
    • First website goes online at CERN
  4. 2000s: Broadband & Beyond
    • Home Internet speeds climb, enabling video and music streaming
    • Cloud services and social media reshape daily life
